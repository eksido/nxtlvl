Part 2: Skills Key

You have now come to the second part of the preparation guide. The Skills Key is a list of specially selected skills that the development dialogue contains.

Skills are about how the employees uses their abilities, knowledge and proficiencies in the daily work tasks. There are two possible development paths; one is to get better at something the employee is already good at and the other is to develop completely new skills that are relevant to the work. Everybody possess the skills that are listed, but it differs to what extent we use and utilise them.

The purpose of the Skills Key is to gain knowledge and understanding of the skills that make the most sense for the employee to develop and focus on in relation to their work.

Preparation based on skills

The preparation is intended to get you and the employee to reflect on your skills in relation to current and future work tasks, so they can get the focus it takes to develop them and solve tasks in an even better way.

Note that the employee does not always have to increase their skills, but they may well be in a situation where they have to ease off their skills, it all depends on their experience, their personality and the nature of the task.
